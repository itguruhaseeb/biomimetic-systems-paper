# Biomimetic Principles in Distributed and AI Systems

[![DOI](https://zenodo.org/badge/DOI/10.5281/zenodo.21366228.svg)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21366228)

A **Perspective and narrative survey** (preprint) on nature-inspired architectures for resilience and efficiency in modern distributed and AI systems.

> **Status:** preprint, not peer reviewed. Prepared for arXiv (cs.DC / cs.NE / cs.SE) and for a later journal/perspective submission.

## What this is

Most bio-inspired computing is presented as a catalog of metaphors. This paper argues the durable value is *structural*. It introduces the **Biomimetic Systems Map (BSM)**, a five-column taxonomy (mechanism, invariant, systems property, engineering analogue, open gap), applies it to five well-grounded mechanism families (stigmergy, spiking computation, adaptive immunity, homeostasis, quorum sensing), and extracts four transferable design invariants: **locality, redundancy, feedback, degeneracy**.

It is deliberately explicit that analogy is a source of hypotheses, not proofs, and it closes with five falsifiable propositions rather than claims.
